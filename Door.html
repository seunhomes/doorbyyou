Measuring & install

Measure the rough opening
  1. For a prehung door system, measure the rough opening — the framed hole in the wall — at its width and height.
  2. Take the width in three places (top, middle, bottom) and use the smallest. Do the same for height.
  3. Standard prehung units need roughly 2"–2½" more than the door slab in both directions for shimming.
Slab-only replacement
  1. Measure the existing slab itself — width, height and thickness — not the frame.
  2. Most steel & fiberglass 36" slabs measure about 35¾" wide and a 6'8" slab about 79"–79¼" tall.
  3. Match the hinge hand (left or right) and bore locations to your existing door.
Thickness & clearance
  1. Standard entry doors are 1¾" thick. Confirm your jamb depth matches the wall.
  2. Allow a sweep gap at the threshold; the bottom seal is not included in slab height.
  3. 8' (96") tall and double-door configurations can be special-ordered in the configurator.
