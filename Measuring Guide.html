Support · How to measure

Measure once,
order with confidence.

Getting the numbers right is the whole game. Follow these three steps and you'll order a door that drops straight into your opening — no surprises at install.

01

Rough opening

For a prehung system, measure the framed hole in the wall — width and height. Take three readings each and use the smallest. Allow about 2"–2½" over the slab size for shimming.

02

Slab replacement

Replacing just the door? Measure the existing slab — most 36" steel and fiberglass slabs are about 35¾" wide and a 6'8" slab about 79" tall. Match the hinge hand and the bore positions.

03

Thickness & hand

Standard entry doors are 1¾" thick — confirm your jamb depth matches the wall. Note the swing direction (left or right hand) so the lock and hinges land on the correct side.

Pro tips

Measure to the framing, not the existing trim or drywall returns.
Check the opening for square by comparing both diagonals — they should match.
The bottom sweep is not included in slab height — leave threshold clearance.
Adding sidelites or a transom? Measure the full multi-unit opening as one.
When in doubt, our team confirms final measurements before production.
